# Scope Boundaries And Red Flags

## Good Fit Signals

- Shopify Plus or growth-stage brand.
- Real storefront pressure: feature velocity, SEO risk, custom UX, performance, app integration, or migration planning.
- Budget can support audit, focused cleanup, or Hydrogen implementation.
- Team understands that custom storefronts need maintenance.
- Buyer wants honest Liquid vs Hydrogen judgment.

## Red Flags

- "We need Hydrogen because it sounds modern."
- No clear commercial pressure.
- Under $1.5K budget for custom storefront work.
- Wants guaranteed ranking or conversion lift.
- Wants a full rebuild without access to analytics, route data, app context, or internal maintenance owner.
- Expects theme-app behavior to transfer automatically into Hydrogen.

## Polite Redirect For Low-Budget Leads

Thanks for the context. Based on the budget and scope, Hydrogen is probably heavier than the problem needs right now. I would use a lighter Shopify theme-focused path first: clean up the current theme, remove app bloat, improve the highest-friction pages, and revisit Hydrogen only if the storefront constraints become commercial blockers.

If you later need a Fit & Risk Audit before a larger decision, that is the safer place for me to help.
